CodingBar Product Landing Page

Redesign the primary school curriculum landing page to align with business goals and target audience.
Use customer research to refine content and visuals, aiming to boost time spent on the page and interaction rates.

BACKDROP

CodingBar is a youth programming education organization, with revenue primarily from its own courses. GA data indicates that visitors spend an average of 1 minute on the course landing page and view about 1.93 pages per session, suggesting limited engagement with the brand.

The target audience is primary school students, with communication extending to their parents. To stand out in a competitive market, we need to redesign the landing page to emphasize our unique value, enhance brand recognition, and improve conversion rates.

OBJECTIVE

Design a landing page that effectively communicates the brand's value, aiming to increase visitor engagement and interaction.

MY ROLE

Participate in Interviews to Understand Customer Profiles and Needs

Assist in developing interview guides and take part in interviews as a recorder and observer. Organize and analyze interview data to understand customer profiles and needs.

Adjust Brand Positioning and Communication Strategy Based on Business Goals and Customer Profiles

Compare target and loyal customer profiles, analyze differences, and provide recommendations for brand image and communication strategies.

Redesign the Brand's Official Website to Convey Corporate Value Proposition

Revise the existing website’s content and visuals based on the new communication strategy. Transform the website into a value bridge between the company and customers, enhancing recognition and fostering further alignment with CodingBar.

DESIGN PROCESS
#1 Customer Research
  • Focus Group： Explore parents' attitudes towards their children's education, views on 3C usage, course selection methods, and challenges. Understand their needs, pain points, behaviors, and motivations.
  • 1 on 1 interview： Understand why loyal customers repeatedly choose CodingBar.
#2 Key Findings
  • Parents' Concerns About Children's Learning Interests and Course Content：
  • They hope to find a personal interest for their child that will encourage deeper learning and skill development.
  • High course prices create uncertainty in their decision-making.
  • Loyal Customers Emphasize on Educational Value, Teaching Expertise, and Course Completeness：
  • Observing course photos on social media and seeing text-based coding (not drag-and-drop blocks) on whiteboards, loyal customers perceive CodingBar’s courses as highly professional compared to competitors.
  • They highlight that CodingBar’s teaching methods inspire their children's interest in learning, encouraging continued engagement.
  • The comprehensive course planning ensures that children won't have their learning interrupted, which is why loyal customers continue to choose CodingBar.
  • Other Concerns：
  • It's challenging to completely prevent children from using 3C devices, yet parents worry about them becoming addicted to gaming and wasting time.
  • Even after reviewing course information, it remains difficult to determine if the course is suitable for their child.
 
#3 Needs Analysis and Function Planning
  • Explore and Inspire Learning Interest

  • Requirement：


    Showcase the diversity and fun of the courses.

  • Design：


    Create a Hero Section to display a variety of courses and a lively atmosphere; include an "Explore Courses" button for interested parents to quickly find course offerings.
  • Emphasize Product Value

  • Requirement：


    Highlight educational advantages and teaching features.

  • Design：


    Create a section to showcase course features.
  • Emphasize Course Planning Completeness

  • Requirement：


    Showcase the completeness of course planning.

  • Design：


    Present course items in a tiered format.
  • Showcase Real Course Conditions

  • Requirement：


    Use visuals to display actual course activities and build trust.

  • Design：


    Create a photo gallery of course activities.
  • Quickly Determine Course Suitability

  • Requirement：


    Help parents assess if the course matches their child's age and level.

  • Design：


    Include course cards with brief descriptions, recommended age, and difficulty level.

#5 Design Decisions
Hero Section
  • Main and subheading clearly communicate the theme and brand value proposition: “Providing professional and diverse cross-disciplinary teaching services to help parents support their children in exploring their interests and talents.”
  • Background use a collage of photos to highlight the variety of CodingBar courses, showcasing classroom activities and the brand's vibrant and engaging style.
 
 
Pain Points and Course Features

  • Highlight Issues:

    Directly address a common concern among parents: “Ensuring children remain adaptable even without a clear future path.”

  • Value Proposition:

    Demonstrate how CodingBar supports parents in helping their children explore interests and discover talents, using a “horizontal exploration, vertical learning” approach to convey both friendly support and professional expertise.

  • Unique Selling Points:

    Outline CodingBar’s unique aspects in three simple steps.

  • Photo Selection:

    Use images to illustrate engaging activities like game-based learning, student-teacher discussions, teamwork, and project presentations. These visuals reinforce the brand's messages of “stimulating learning motivation through fun activities,” “professional and patient teaching,” and a brand tone that is “professional, lively, and friendly.”
 
 
Course List

  • Tabs:

    Allow parents to quickly switch and view different course options, reducing the inconvenience of scrolling through a long list on mobile devices.

  • Multi-Stage Options:

    Offer a range from one-day trials to basic and advanced courses, addressing customer needs for hands-on experience before committing. This reduces decision-making stress and encourages initial interaction with CodingBar.

  • Course Information Cards:

    Display essential details such as course thumbnail, name, target audience, skill requirements, description, and price. This helps parents quickly assess if a course suits their child. Interested users can click on the cards to view detailed course information.
 
 
Activity Photo Gallery

  • Carousel:

    Display course activity photos in a horizontal scrolling carousel to highlight CodingBar’s dynamic, professional, and enjoyable learning environment.

  • Photo Content:

    Include images of instructors explaining complex code in simple terms, providing assistance, student interactions, and offline activities. Address common parent concerns:
  • What is the teaching format? Is CodingBar professional?
  • What is the student-to-teacher ratio? Will my child be overlooked in larger classes?
  • Will programming be too dull and difficult to follow?
  • Are there breaks? We want to avoid excessive screen time for children.

  • Brand Building:

    Use the photo gallery to enhance brand image and build trust.
 
 
Professional Endorsements
  • Combine emotional appeal with rational credibility. Below the engaging photo gallery, showcase CodingBar’s awards and partnerships from both domestic and international sources to communicate the brand’s “professionalism and reliability” to customers.
