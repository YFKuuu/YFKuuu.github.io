BSKINI App

Identify the intersection between AI technology development and customer needs by conducting market analysis, creating Lo-Fi Wireframes and flowcharts, and employing Scrum methodology to rapidly design and develop an AI-driven diet tracking app.

BACKDROP

Dishrank is dedicated to providing healthy eating services by combining professional nutritionist information with digital recommendations for healthy meals. Since incorporating AI technology in 2019, the focus has shifted to developing AI-assisted diet tracking services.

Target User： Working professionals aged 25 ~ 45 who value health and body management but have limited time. The diet tracking app aims to help them manage eating habits, increase health awareness, and efficiently achieve weight management goals.

OBJECTIVE

Design a simple and efficient diet tracking app.

MY ROLE

Collaborate with the PM to gather market information and analysis, confirm product strategy, and design user flows and interfaces based on customer needs.

RESULTS & IMPACT

Collaborated with the PM and development team, creating user flow diagrams and Lo-Fi wireframes rapidly. Using Scrum methodology, we minimized communication costs and information gaps, successfully completing the product functionality definition, design, and development within 3 months.

CHALLENGES
  • Finding a suitable product strategy that aligns with the company’s goal of developing AI image recognition for nutritional analysis while meeting market user needs and expanding technical resources.
  • Identifying a unique market value proposition amid numerous existing diet tracking and management products, such as MyFitnessPal with exercise integration and Cofit with live nutritionist consultations.
  • Maintaining a good user experience for diet tracking before achieving exceptional performance with AI image recognition.
DESIGN PROCESS
#1 User Research
  • Secondary Research： Analyzing online data to understand diet management needs of office workers.
  • First-Hand Experience： Team members (3+) used weight management services such as nutritionist meal plans and gym weight loss programs; shared personal insights and challenges in daily stand-up meetings.
#2 User Pain Points and Needs Analysis
  • Complex Personal Data Setup：
  • Key weight management data include gender, age, height, weight, and activity level. The process often requires switching keyboards for different data types and fields, which can be frustrating due to constant keyboard changes.
  • Cumbersome Recording Process：
  • Major products like MyFitnessPal and FatSecret often feature databases of foreign brands and foods, lacking common local options like those from breakfast shops or convenience stores. Users must manually break down, search, adjust portions, and record local food items.
  • Professionals recommend taking photos to avoid missing information, but adding photos makes the recording process more tedious, and users still need to manually enter nutritional details.
  • Large Goals Can Lead to Disengagement：
  • Achieving healthy weight management often takes 1 to 6 months. Without strong motivation (e.g., a wedding), users may lose interest if they don’t see immediate results.
  • Maintaining weight loss requires ongoing dietary control, which can be a significant long-term challenge.
#3 Product Strategy

Become the leading service provider with the most extensive database of Taiwanese dishes.

  • Analyze and create a nutritional database for common Taiwanese dishes. In the first phase, focus on the Neihu district, targeting high-density office areas, collecting menus and dish images from local restaurants, and having a nutritionist analyze and build the database.
  • Integrate government data to compile nutritional information for chain restaurants across Taiwan.
  • Develop a food portion calculation formula and use AI image recognition technology to enable quick photo-based recording.
#4 Design Decisions

Basic Information and Goals

  • Categorize basic data to streamline the data entry process with consistent operations.
  • Design a "Custom Plan Segments" feature to break down large goals into smaller, more achievable stages.
  • Provide before-and-after comparisons to motivate users.
▲ Reduce the need for switching between various keyboards during complex basic data setup.

Photo-to-Record and Simplified Logging with Pop-ups

  • Use AI image recognition for recording to reduce multiple clicks and streamline the logging process.
  • In AI image recognition, provide a pop-up with similar items for quick adjustments and record creation.
▲ Within a certain recognition accuracy range, use pop-ups to display similar recognition results, allowing users to quickly replace the content.

Streamline Manual Dietary Record Creation with Tabs

  • Tabs help users quickly switch between categories during manual record creation, reducing the need for repetitive searches. Categories include keyword search, brand types, custom entries, and historical records.

Clear Record Dashboard for Quick Dietary Overview

  • Intuitive graphics show the relationship between current dietary status and goals, providing clear guidance for meal choices.
  • Time-Based Tracking: Record meals by different time periods to account for variations in calorie absorption throughout the day.
▲ Display carbon, protein, fat, and water intake percentages to help users quickly understand their current dietary status and easily adjust meal choices.

Phase-Based Progress Display

  • The personal report page shows calorie intake and weight changes, along with basic information, countdown timers, and weight loss goals.
  • Users can view records and body changes segmented by week, month, and quarter.
  • Staged weight loss goals enhance achievement feelings and make data changes more noticeable, boosting user confidence in reaching their goals.

Post-Plan Support

  • At the end of a plan, users can choose between maintaining weight or starting a new weight loss plan to continue managing their diet.
  • If the weight loss goal is not met, users can reset and begin a new weight loss plan.
