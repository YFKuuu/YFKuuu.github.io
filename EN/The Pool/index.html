Spring Pool Glass Studio Circular Economy Service Design

Develop a circular economy brand and its business model by leveraging service innovation. Create design guidelines to drive continuous innovation within the enterprise.

BACKDROP

Spring Pool Glass Studio, founded by Mr. Wu Chun-Chi, has been dedicated to sustainable glass recycling for nearly 50 years. Located in Hsinchu, the studio has weathered the downturn of the Hsinchu glass industry in the 1980s.

Seeking to foster a new generation and make sustainability a practical reality, Spring Pool envisioned "The Pool." The first site for this concept was chosen next to the old Hsinchu Glass Art Museum in Hsinchu Park, an abandoned glass workshop, to create a space where sustainability could thrive beyond just being a slogan.

OBJECTIVE

Starting from The Pool’s location in Hsinchu Park, explore the surrounding area to develop innovative business models and circular economy service design guidelines through field analysis, case studies, and conceptual design.

MY ROLE

Conducting Interviews and Compiling Information

To advance the second phase of service concept design and develop potential business models, I participated in interviews with potential collaborators at Hsinchu Park during the project's initial stage. I compiled the gathered information, which served as essential reference material for developing service concepts.

Planning and Facilitating Workshops

Based on customer profiles and various circular economy case studies, I designed and organized workshops for The Pool, focusing on circular economy service concepts and activities.

CHALLENGES

The Pool is tasked with implementing and promoting a circular economy. However, the circular economy itself is not a single industry but an operational model spanning various industries and economic activities. Its promotion is quite complex and requires designing different production and business models tailored to the specifics of each industry to effectively integrate circular economy principles.

成果與影響
  • The design concepts presented during the workshop left a lasting impression on the project lead, Wu Ting-An, from Spring Pool.
  • The Glass Token service experience has been continuously applied by Spring Pool in various activities, including the "2022__Galas," "The Pool｜Tainan," and even "Golden Horse Awards 60."
  • The circular economy service design guidelines have provided a foundation for innovation in similar industries.
DESIGN PROCESS
#1 Key Stakeholder Interviews

Interviews were conducted with The Pool and other businesses within Hsinchu Park.

The interview framework focused on "customer profiles," "company strengths and marketing strategies," "current service operations," and "attitudes toward circular economy concepts," aiming to explore service concept development and future business collaboration opportunities.

#2 Service Concept Co-Creation Workshop I

After compiling various circular service case studies, we identified key elements for promoting circular economy concepts: "circular stages," "behavior change methods," and "objectives." By combining these elements, numerous creative ideas emerged.

In the first co-creation workshop, we provided The Pool team with service concept idea cards and service blueprints. Together, we explored the customer journey—from before they arrive, during their experience, to after they leave—and identified ways to connect each touchpoint with circular economy principles.

#3 Concept Evaluation

We compiled the service ideas generated from the first co-creation workshop and recruited participants for interviews to gather feedback on these concepts. The goal was to understand customers' attitudes and opinions: Do they find the concepts interesting? What might deter them?

▲ We recruited three participants from each of the two target groups for The Pool to gauge their thoughts on the service concepts and use their feedback to refine the service design.
▲ Participants provided valuable insights and considerations related to the circular economy principles embedded in the service concepts.
#4 Service Concept Co-Creation Workshop II

In the second co-creation workshop, in addition to The Pool partners, we also invited teams involved in designing and planning service experiences. This included space manager San Miao, dining designer Mu Geng, and exhibition partner Peng Ding. The workshop aimed to build consensus among the different teams and incorporate diverse perspectives to make the service concepts more concrete and feasible.
