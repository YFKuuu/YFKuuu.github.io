Noise Eraser App

Based on the wireframe provided by the client, enhance the user experience through UI and micro-interaction design within the existing framework and timeline.

BACKDROP

DeepWave is dedicated to enabling computers to understand and process environmental sounds just like humans. Their first product, Noise Eraser (web version), is an audio tool designed to automatically reduce noise in sound and video files while preserving human voices. It is primarily used in the news media industry to enhance audio processing for interview recordings, removing noises like fan hum, sudden dog barks, and environmental echoes.

To better align with real-world applications, DeepWave will soon launch an app version.

OBJECTIVE

Enhance the user experience through UI and micro-interaction design within the existing framework and timeline.

MY ROLE

Optimize the user experience and design the UI according to customer requirements and the wireframe, collaborating with the development team to complete the App 1.0 development tasks.

RESULTS & IMPACT

After the launch of the Noise Eraser App, it was awarded the Excellence Award in the Lifestyle category at the 2023 Asia Smart APP Award.

DESIGN FEATURE
#1 Adding a Touch of Warmth and Charm to the Cold, Rigid Technolog

Due to the product's clear functional goals and simple steps, the straightforward functionality made the app feel monotonous and impersonal.

To address this, I designed a character based on the product's name — Eraser Man, embodying the concept of "erasing the unwanted." This character was integrated into the UI design, adding a playful and lively touch. This cute and engaging character not only set the app apart from other similar, tech-heavy products but also helped the app win Asia Smart APP Award in the Lifestyle category in 2023.。

#2 Adjusting Design Layout for More Meaningful Information

After importing media files into the database, users need to review and confirm content before uploading selected files to the AI noise reduction workshop.

Understanding the goals and workflow from the client's provided Wireframe, we proposed changing the layout of cards from vertical to horizontal. This adjustment allows more information to be displayed at the same height, making it more user-friendly for users on shorter devices as well.

▲ When users select multiple audiovisual files for import, the list displays information for users to confirm the files awaiting processing.
#3 Focus Users on Key Tasks Without Changing the Workflow!

After uploading files to the AI Noise Reduction Workshop, they are categorized into four states: "Uploading," "Analyzing Processing," "Awaiting Analysis," and "Processing Completed." Users can freely adjust noise reduction styles, ratios, etc., after processing is complete, and then download or share the files.

To align with the client's established workflow, we recommend highlighting the "Processing Completed" area prominently. This section should feature larger cards and allow users to swipe left or right to view files, with a narrow horizontal list below for categorizing file statuses using labels. By adjusting the layout in this manner, users can remain focused on their primary tasks while still having comprehensive visibility of secondary information.

▲ The top half of the page displays analyzed audio files in large card format, where users can click to freely adjust personalized parameters, share, or download the files.
