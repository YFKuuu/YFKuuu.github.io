CodingBar Course Marketing

Designed and developed marketing strategies for STEAM courses targeting elementary and middle school students. Implemented a cycle of online and offline marketing activities, resulting in a 47.8% increase in annual enrollments.

BACKDROP

CodingBar is a youth coding education institution offering IT skills courses for elementary to high school students.

Roblox, a popular game among elementary students, uses the Lua programming language and allows users to create and share games. CodingBar, ahead of competitors, has developed exclusive Roblox camp courses to spark young students' interest in IT through their favorite game.

OBJECTIVE

Objective

Design short-term experience activities to increase product exposure, attract parents to bring their children for a trial, and ultimately drive enrollment in winter camp courses.

Plan

  • Host experience activities on weekends during the off-season from September to December.
  • Use advertisements to boost product exposure and create brand impressions and connections with parents.

CHALLENGES

Seasonal Enrollment Variability

CodingBar's primary revenue comes from winter and summer camps. While summer camps perform well due to the long break, winter camps face challenges due to the shorter, fragmented holiday period. Ensuring sufficient winter camp enrollments has been a persistent challenge.

Low Customer Awareness

Many parents are aware of their children's obsession with Roblox but lack understanding of its educational potential. Without knowledge that Roblox has a Studio for creating games using 3D construction and programming, parents are unlikely to seek out or choose CodingBar's courses proactively.

RESULTS & IMPACT
  • From September 2023, held 17 Roblox one-day camp events with a total of 142 students.
  • The 2024 winter camp saw 34 enrollments, a 47.8% increase compared to the same period last year, with 38% of enrollments coming from one-day camp participants.
INSIGHTS & OBSERVATIONS
#1 Activity Theme is a Key Parameter

Based on prior user interviews, we learned that parents consider their children's interests and opinions when selecting activities.

In our initial design of the experience camp, we planned two different themes: "Parkour" and "Treasure Island." Testing these themes with the same advertising budget and copy revealed :

  • The "Parkour" ad attracted more attention than the "Treasure Island" ad, with the former's exposure and click-through rates being twice as high.
  • The disparity in registrations was even more significant, with "Parkour" receiving 4.8 times more sign-ups than "Treasure Island" within the same advertising period.
▲ Ads for the "Parkour" theme outperformed those for the "Treasure island" creation game in both ad appeal and actual course attraction.
#2 Video Ads Outperformed Static Images; Teaching Contexts Surpassed Roblox Themes

For the "Parkour" theme, as the ad campaign progressed, fatigue set in and effectiveness declined. We tested various materials to refresh the campaign：

  • Video ads were 2-3 times more engaging than static images, reducing CPC from NT$6 to NT$4.
  • Ads featuring teaching interactions and learning scenarios were more effective in encouraging parents to learn about the brand.

#3 Continuously Creating Interaction to Retain Customers

In the small-scale tests conducted in July and August, the "Parkour" theme proved more attractive. By September, we expanded to five cities: Taipei, New Taipei, Hsinchu, Taichung, and Kaohsiung.

Each event was an opportunity to understand our customers better and gather feedback from students：

  • Parents worries about violent content in online games.
  • However, children show great interest in the Roblox game "Doors."
  • Beyond parkour games, kids also have a strong enthusiasm for shooting games.

Based on the insights, we designed a new experience course titled "Foggy Forest" with a dark, suspenseful theme and shooting elements. Launching in October, this course allows students who previously attended the "Parkour" experience to return, stay engaged with the brand, and increase their interest in the winter camp.

▲ Launched for Halloween, "Foggy Forest" features a suspenseful yet non-violent theme, fulfilling children's desires and reassuring parents.
#4 Winning Parents with Children's Smiles

Marketing courses for children is challenging as the buyer and the participant are different.

Parents usually drop off their kids and leave, missing out on the excitement of the class. Though they often ask their children about their experience, kids typically struggle to explain it fully. To help parents appreciate CodingBar's value, we designed each event to：

  • Invite parents back 15 minutes before the end to watch their child's presentation and review session.
  • Design the presentation so that everyone plays each student's game, creating a sense of achievement and shared fun.
  • Conduct a quick review to showcase the day's key skills and demonstrate the child’s progress.
  • Document the event and create a personalized album linked with student projects, sending it to parents to turn their investment into memorable keepsakes.
